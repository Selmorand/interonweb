Schema Markup Explained

Schema markup is structured data that tells machines facts about your website. It's like a nutrition label for your web pages, providing clear, standardised information that search engines and AI systems can read and understand.

The Nutrition Label Analogy

Imagine trying to understand what's in food by only reading the marketing on the package. "Delicious! Natural! Family favourite!" tells you nothing useful.

The nutrition label, however, provides facts in a standard format: calories, protein, carbohydrates. Any system can read and compare these values.

Schema markup does the same thing for websites. Instead of AI systems guessing what your business does from marketing text, schema provides explicit facts in a format machines understand.

What Schema Looks Like

Schema markup is typically added as JSON-LD (JavaScript Object Notation for Linked Data) in your website's code. Here's a simple example:

<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "Cape Town Plumbing",
  "description": "Emergency and residential plumbing services",
  "@type": "Plumber",
  "telephone": "+27 21 555 0123",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "addressLocality": "Cape Town",
    "addressCountry": "ZA"
  },
  "areaServed": "Cape Town Metropolitan Area",
  "priceRange": "$$"
}
</script>

This tells machines exactly what this business is, where it operates, and how to contact it.

Critical Schema Types for Businesses

Schema Type Purpose
Organization Basic business identity: name, logo, contact info, social profiles
LocalBusiness Physical businesses: location, hours, service area
Service What services you offer with descriptions
Product Products you sell with prices and availability
FAQPage Questions and answers AI can extract directly
Article Blog posts and content pieces

Important Properties

knowsAbout

Lists topics your organization has expertise in. This helps AI systems understand your areas of knowledge:

"knowsAbout": [
  "Residential Plumbing",
  "Emergency Repairs",
  "Geyser Installation",
  "Drain Cleaning"
]

hasOfferCatalog

Groups your services or products together, making it easy for AI to understand everything you offer:

"hasOfferCatalog": {
  "@type": "OfferCatalog",
  "name": "Plumbing Services",
  "itemListElement": [...]
}

areaServed

Specifies where your business operates, critical for local search and AI recommendations:

"areaServed": {
  "@type": "City",
  "name": "Cape Town"
}

Why Schema Matters for AI

When someone asks ChatGPT "Who can fix a burst pipe in Sandton?", the AI looks for businesses that:

  1. Clearly identify as plumbers (schema type)
  2. List pipe repair as a service
  3. Serve the Sandton area

Without schema, AI systems have to guess these facts from your content. With schema, they know for certain.
