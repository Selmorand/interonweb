SEO Fundamentals

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the technical foundation that enables both search engines and AI systems to find and understand your website. While AI Readiness goes beyond traditional SEO, you can't have good AI Readiness without solid SEO fundamentals.

Why SEO Still Matters

Some people wonder if SEO is still relevant with AI changing search. The answer: absolutely. AI systems still rely on many of the same signals:

  • They need to be able to crawl and access your content
  • They use metadata to understand page purpose
  • They rely on heading structure to understand content hierarchy
  • They consider technical factors like speed and mobile-friendliness

Think of SEO as the foundation of a house. You can't add fancy features without a solid foundation.

Key SEO Elements We Check

Title Tags

The title tag tells search engines and AI what your page is about. It appears in browser tabs and search results.

  • Should be 50-60 characters
  • Should clearly describe page content
  • Should be unique for each page

Meta Descriptions

A summary of your page content that appears in search results.

  • Should be 120-160 characters
  • Should accurately summarise the page
  • Should include relevant information for users

Heading Structure

Headings (H1, H2, H3) create a hierarchy that helps machines understand your content structure.

  • One H1 per page (your main title)
  • H2s for main sections
  • H3s for subsections
  • Logical, hierarchical structure

HTTPS

Secure connection is mandatory. Search engines penalise non-HTTPS sites, and users don't trust them.

Mobile-Friendliness

Your site must work well on mobile devices. Most searches now happen on mobile.

Page Speed

Slow sites frustrate users and rank poorly. Key metrics include:

  • Time to first byte
  • Largest contentful paint
  • Cumulative layout shift

SEO vs AI Readiness

SEO Covers AI Readiness Adds
Technical crawlability Structured data (schema)
Keyword optimization Machine-readable facts
Link building AI-quotable content
Ranking in search results Being cited in AI answers

Both matter. SEO is necessary but no longer sufficient on its own.
